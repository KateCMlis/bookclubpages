About Hennepin County Library Book Club Kits

Hennepin County Library has over one hundred fifty titles available to book clubs to check out a in a book club kit. All titles are available in print format, while some books also have multiple copies available in the Hennepin County Library system in audio and ebook formats. Book groups can check out a book club kit for their group's meeting.

  1. Book club kits contain 8-10 copies of a single title.
  2. Kits are checked out for six weeks and may not be renewed.
  3. A kit is checked out to one patron who is responsible for all of the materials. Fees for missing books will be charged to the patron who checked out the kit.
  4. Kits must be returned to a Hennepin County library service desk during open hours and cannot be returned in a book drop.
  5. You can place a hold on a kit and have it sent to any Hennepin County library location for pickup.
  6. If a kit is checked in, you may pick it up at Southdale Library. Call Southdale Library to have staff confirm its availability and to hold the kit for you.

Book Club Resources

If you are thinking about starting your own book club, have the role leading your existing book club's meetings, or are a new member of a club that has existed for a while and want some information about what to expect at a meeting, there are resources aplenty online to help you out. Here are some places to get started:

  1. In collaboration with Minneapolis Star Tribune columnist Myron Medcalf, Hennepin County Library hosts the Mary Ann Key Book Club It is dedicated to "reading as a catalyst for conversation" about themes of "past and current injustices including systemic racism, other forms of discrimination, and bias that affects marginalized communities" ("Book Clubs," n.d.).
  2. I Love Libraries, an initiative of the American Library Association, provides a wealth of information on how to start, host, and participate in a book club.
  3. The website Book Riot has many terrific resources for people interested in book clubs. Start with their advice podcast "Dear Book Nerd," which has episode devoted to book clubs. You can also check out Book Riot's article on how to find a book club to join. Or, their article on how to start your own book club as well as how to reboot a book club that has existed for a while and needs to be refreshed.
  4. If you are interested in participating in an online book club, Wired Magazine and The Observer Magazine offer some tips on how to find or start your own.
